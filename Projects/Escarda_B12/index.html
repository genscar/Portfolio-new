AUTHENTIC BACOLODNON CUISINE



Bacolod City known as "The City of Smiles," is renowned for its MassKara Festival and its delicious local delicacies like Piaya, Cansi, Napoleones, and Chicken Inasal.

Check out everything Bacolod has to offer in this travel vlog!

TRAVELLER'S TOP PICKS


Azucarera


Azucarera, a Filipino-Spanish heritage restaurant, is a collaboration between Dos Mestizos and Bob's. It serves delicious, intentionally-made Filipino-Spanish dishes like callos con garbanzo, paella negra, and pollo al ajillo. The restaurant offers a diverse selection of wines and a cocktail menu, offering a modern take on Spanish dining in Bacolod.

Rating:

Gina’s Seafood


Gina's Seafood is a popular restaurant for fish and shellfish lovers, offering a variety of flavorful dishes like fish, shrimp, squid, and crab. With a set menu and a unique dining experience, it's perfect for first-timers and picky eaters.

Rating:

Pepe’s + Balboa


Pepe's + Balboa offers a variety of cuisines, including traditional Pinoy comfort food, American favorites, and Italian classics. Their dishes range from PHP200 to PHP350, making them a great choice for large groups or personal meals, making it a reliable dining option.

Rating:

Chicken House


Chicken House in Bacolod is renowned for its chicken inasal, a popular Filipino dish. Serving tender, grilled chicken classics since 1976, they offer bestsellers like paa, pecho, and isol, all served with garlic rice and rich chicken oil.

Rating:

Diotay's Eatery


Diotay's Seafood Eatery allows customers to select desired fish, crabs, or seafood, and choose cooking or seafood dishes. The most interesting fish species are bailer shells, used by fishermen to "bail" out water.


Rating:

Bob's Restaurant


Bob's Restaurant Bacolod, established in 1965, offers a diverse menu of American and Filipino dishes, catering to both locals and visitors. The family business is known for its innovation and is a popular spot for family gatherings and special occasions. Popular dishes include baby back ribs, sate babe, salads, and lasagna.

Rating:

21 Restaurant

21 Restaurant in Bacolod offers homecooked Filipino food like adobong alimusan, lumpia, and sinigang, perfect for large groups, providing a warm, welcoming atmosphere for dining.


Rating:

Calea

Calea is a popular dessert spot in Bacolod City, known for its rich, decadent cakes. The small coffee shop and bakery offers a variety of flavors, including famous chocolate cake, Mud Pie, and classic blueberry cheesecake, ensuring a memorable sugar rush.

Rating:

Aboy's Restaurant


Aboy's Restaurant in Bacolod is a culinary icon, showcasing the rich food culture of Negros Occidental. The family-owned establishment represents Bacolod's love for gastronomic adventures and Filipino hospitality. The menu features mouthwatering dishes like chicken inasal, kansi, batchoy, and sugba-sugba, showcasing the region's culinary heritage.

Rating:
